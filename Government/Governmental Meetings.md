A **Governmental Meeting** is a meeting which [[Elected Officials]] [[Elected Officials#Call for Governmental Meetings|can call for]] in order to make decisions.

During the meeting, all available officials meet and vote on a choice that must be made. There must be at least 2 available [[Elected Officials]] or a **Governmental Meeting** cannot be held. 
If a draw is decided, then the choice is the "yes", "go ahead" or "prosecutor wins" answer.

[[Citizen|Citizens]] who are not [[Elected Officials]] can sit in on **Governmental Meetings** but they cannot participate in voting unless the **Governmental Meeting**.

# Citizen Meeting
A **Citizen Meeting** is a meeting where all [[Citizen|Citizens]] are given an opportunity of at least 1 [[Taxes#Tax Period|Tax Period]] to vote for or against something.

A [[Citizen]] must be given the opportunity to vote remotely. The online status of a [[Citizen]] doesn't matter. [[Citizen|Citizens]] must vote "yea" or "nay" or are not considered in the vote.

If 2/3rds of [[Citizen|Citizens]] vote yea then the voted subject is approved/passed else it fails/is denied. In the case that exactly 2/3rds of [[Citizen|Citizens]] vote yea the vote fails/is denied.
