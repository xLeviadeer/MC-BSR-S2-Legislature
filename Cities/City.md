A **City** is a zone of land within [[Land#Hard Land|Hard Land]] and sometimes [[Land#Soft Land|Soft Land]] which is primarily managed by a [[City Manager]] under the oversight of [[Elected Officials]] which maintains several [[#Core Objectives]]. A **City** is composed of [[Property Co-Owner#Co-Owned Property|Co-Owned Property]] of which the [[Government]] or [[Property Owner|Property Owners]] can be leased [[Land]] under the national [[Jurisdiction]] of the [[Government]] and additionally the **City**.

---
## Core Objectives
**Core Objectives** are generic questions which each **City** must answer before it is founded and which are maintained throughout the management of the **City** by the [[City Manager]].
#### Purpose
Why — **City** [[Land]] has reduced [[Taxes]]. The [[Government]] requires a purpose for why a **City** should exist and not be as heavily taxed on. 
Question — What the point of your **City** is. How is it different from existing **Citities**? What does your **City** solve?
#### Architecture
Why — A **City** is intended to have a distinct architecture and set of rules on how the architecture can be in a given **City**.
Question — What is the style of your **City**? How do the architecture choices help to support your [[#Purpose]]?
#### Rules
Why — A **City** often requires rules that govern what [[Citizen|Citizens]] can or cannot do within a **City**. A **City's** rules cannot eclipse or conflict [[Government|Governmental]] [[Jurisdiction]].
Question — What rules govern your **City**? How do the rules support the wellbeing of **City** [[Citizen|Citizens]]? How do the rules help to support your [[#Purpose]]?
#### Rights
Why — A **City** often requires rights that govern what [[Citizen|Citizens]] gain by being in a **City**. A **City's** rights cannot eclipse or conflict [[Government|Governmental]] [[Jurisdiction]].
Question — What rights do [[Citizen|Citizens]] of your **City** have? Why should [[Citizen|Citizens]] choose to be here? How do the rights help to support your [[#Purpose]]?
#### Landarea
Why — The [[Government]] must know where the bounds of your **City** is and ensure it does not encroach on other [[Land]]. 
Question — How much space will your **City** take up? Where are the boundaries? Why do you need this [[Land]]? How does this [[Land]] help to support your [[#Purpose]]?
#### Manager
Why — Each **City** must have a manager who takes responsibility for the [[Citizen|Citizens]] in the **City**.
Question — Who ⧼will⧽ manage this **City**? Why will this person manage the **City**?

## City Rules
#### Deconstruction
A **City** may *not* be deconstructed after approved foundation unless it is by agreement of the [[City Manager]] and approval of a [[Governmental Meetings|Governmental Meeting]]. Once a **City** is deconstructed, [[Land]] is allocated as the [[Property]] of the [[City Manager]] who can relay ownership to individual [[Property Owner|Property Owners]] in the **City** who Co-Own [[Property|Properties]] respectively.

A **City** *may* be forcefully deconstructed if the **City** fails to comply with [[Jurisdiction]] or [[#City Rules]].  
#### Declarative Land
A **City's** [[Property]] is not subject to [[Purchasing Land|land purchasing costs]] but rather ⸉declared⸉ (by [[City Manager#Request Land Expansions|expansion request]]) by the [[City Manager]] with approval for an [[Elected Officials#Elected Official|Elected Official]] via a [[Governmental Meetings|Governmental Meeting]].
#### Clear Land Boundary
A **City** must give a clear [[Land]] boundary for when entering the **City** so that any [[#Rules]] and [[#Rights]] may be implied while within the area.
#### Streets
**Streets** are a portion of a **City** used for transportation. **Streets** are maintained by the **City**. **Streets** must be [[Property#Publicized (Portions of) Private Property|Publicized Property]] with the purpose of transporting [[Citizen|Citizens]] throughout a **City**.
#### Trappage of Citizens
A **City** cannot trap [[Citizen|Citizens]] or make it difficult for [[Citizen|Citizens]] to enter or leave the **City**. This includes creating [[Property]] restrictions which would cause [[Citizen|Citizens]] to only be able to proceed by accepting some type of [[Contracts|Contract]], [[Fines|Fine]] or other negative effect.
#### Public Communications Center
A **City** must have a **Public Communications Center (PCC)** where [[Citizen|Citizens]] can send letters or otherwise communicate with the [[City Manager]]. This must be [[Property#Publicized (Portions of) Private Property|Publicized Property]]. The **PCC** may include a [[#Public Board]] inside of it. There is a 10 [[Taxes#Tax Period|Tax Period]] grace period for the **PCC** to be created after founding a **City**.
#### Public Board
A **City** must have a **Public Board (PB)** where [[Citizen|Citizens]] can view any [[#Rules]] &𝓇 [[#Rights]] that this **City** uses as well as any updates to the [[#Rules]] &𝓇 [[#Rights]]. There is a 10 [[Taxes#Tax Period|Tax Period]] grace period for the **PB** to be created after founding a **City**.
#### Anti-Land Mutilation Ignorance
**Cities** ignore the [[Purchasing Land#Land Anti-Mutilation Incentive|Land Anti-Mutilation Incentive]] within their bounds.
