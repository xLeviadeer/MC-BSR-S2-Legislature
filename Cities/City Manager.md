## City Manager Rules
#### Partial Enforcement Responsibility for Property Co-Owner Rules
A **City Manager** is expected to participate and play the primary role in enforcing [[Property Co-Owner#Co-Owned Property|Co-Owned Property]] rules and rights. A **City Manager** can call in the [[Government]] for enforcement if needed.
#### Maximum Allowed Managed Cities
One [[Citizen]] cannot manage more than 3 [[City|Cities]].
#### Responsibility of Citizens
A **City Manager** takes the primary responsibility of [[Citizen]] wrong-doings within their [[City]] of [[Citizen|Citizens]] whose [[Government#Request for or Issuing of a Primary Residence|primary residence]] is within the [[City]] assuming that said wrong-doings are the fault of negligence in [[Jurisdiction]] enforcement or [[City#City Rules|City Rule]] enforcement.
#### Notification of Rule Changes
A **City Manager** must notify [[Citizen|Citizens]] of [[City#Rules|City Rule]] changes at the [[City]]'s [[City#Public Board|Public Board]].
#### Compliance with Governmental Requests
A **City Manager** should do their best to comply with reasonable [[Government|Governmental]] requests.
#### Governmental Eviction
A **City Manager** may be evicted as a **City Manager** from their [[City]] if the **City Manager** fails to pay [[Taxes]] on their [[City]] more than 3 times consecutively. The **City Manager** must be given a 10 [[Taxes#Tax Period|Tax Period]] notice before eviction takes place. During eviction, the [[Government]] seizes control of a [[City]] and will act as a group **City Manager** for this [[City]] unless otherwise sold or appointed by the [[Government]].

## City Manager Rights
#### Request Land Expansions
A **City Manger** can request an expansion of [[City]] [[Land]] through a [[Elected Officials#Elected Official|Elected Official]].
#### Appoint New Managers & Step Down
A **City Manager** can step down from being a **City Manager** but is required to either appoint a new **City Manager** who accepts the role or donate the [[City]] to the [[Government]] who will act as a group **City Manager** for this [[City]] unless otherwise sold or appointed by the [[Government]]. This can only be done with the **City Manager** giving a 10 [[Taxes#Tax Period|Tax Period]] notice.
#### Lease City Property
A **City Manager** holds the sole right to lease out [[City]] [[Property Co-Owner#Co-Owned Property|Co-Owned Properties]] to [[Citizen|Citizens]] and negotiate terms of leasing the agreement.

[[Land]] rent for [[Property Co-Owner#Co-Owned Property|Co-Owned Property]] must be a value which can be viewed while the **City Manager** is offline and is still due for tenant [[Property Co-Owner]]s while the **City Manager** is offline.
#### Eviction of Tenants
A **City Manager** can evict [[Property Co-Owner]]'s from their [[City]] with a 10 [[Taxes#Tax Period|Tax Period]] notice before eviction. After which, the [[Property Co-Owner#Co-Owned Property|Co-Owned Property]] is legally claimed by the **City Manager**.
#### Approve City Structures
A **City Manager** can approve the construction of a building falling out of range according to the rule on the [[City#City Provision Requirement for Buildings Outside of y80 to y120|Governmental Provision Requirement for Buildings Outside of y80 to y120]]. Buildings higher than y200 must be approved via a [[Governmental Meetings|Governmental Meeting]]. 
#### Entitle Individuals to Work on Behalf of the City Manager
A **City Manager** may entitle [[Citizen|Citizens]] to work on behalf of the **City Manager** in their absence or as part of their workforce. Ultimately, the wrong-doings of these individuals still may fall onto the **City Manager** for improper management.
#### Enforcing Citizens to Participate Collaborative Assignments
**City Managers** are allowed to enforce the participation of [[Assignments#Collaborative Assignments|Collaborative Assignments]] on [[Citizen|Citizens]] whose [[Government#Request for or Issuing of a Primary Residence|primary residence]] is in the [[City]]. A **City Manager**╌however╌cannot enforce completion.
*This rule is not valid if the [[City]] is solely within [[Land#Soft Land|Soft Land]].
